Environmental Importance

Trees provide oxygen, regulate climate, and support wildlife. Forests create habitats for countless species and balance the health of our planet’s ecosystems. They store carbon, stabilize soils, and protect watersheds that supply drinking water. The complex relationships within a forest — from fungi and insects to birds and mammals — show how essential trees are to the structure and function of the entire ecosystem.
